The Rules of the Bed
Stimulus control — rebuilding the bed-sleep association. Follow these consistently.
🛏️
Bed is for sleep and sex onlyNo reading, scrolling, watching, worrying, or working in bed. If you do these things in bed, your brain learns the bed = wakefulness.
🚶
Get up if you can't sleepIf awake for ~20 minutes, get out of bed. Go to another room. Do something calm and boring. Return only when genuinely sleepy.
⏰
Same wake time every dayYour rise time anchors your circadian rhythm. Sleeping in on weekends — even 1 hour — unravels progress. This is the hardest rule and the most important.
🚫
No naps (for now)Napping bleeds off sleep pressure, making it harder to fall asleep at night. Once your sleep consolidates, brief naps may be reintroduced.
🌙
Don't try to sleepSleep is passive — it happens when you stop trying. Practice quiet wakefulness instead. "I'll just rest here" is more effective than "I must sleep NOW."

Brain Dump
Can't sleep because your mind is racing? Empty it here. Getting thoughts out of your head and onto "paper" signals your brain it doesn't need to keep holding them.
Paradoxical Intention
Try to stay awake with your eyes open. Seriously. The pressure to fall asleep is often what prevents it — removing the goal removes the anxiety.
💡
Lie in bed in the dark. Keep your eyes open softly. Your only goal is to stay awake while remaining still. Don't fight sleep — just don't chase it. Most people fall asleep within minutes.
Cognitive Shuffling
A technique to quiet the problem-solving part of your brain. Visualize random, unconnected images in sequence — this mimics the semi-random imagery of early sleep onset.
